# Competitors

One profile per competitor, plus a comparison matrix. In Disciplined Entrepreneurship the
competitive view is **customer-centric**: what matters is how you rank against alternatives
on the **two priorities your target persona cares about most** (Step 11), and where your
**Core** (Step 10) makes you hard to copy. The "competitor" a customer actually compares
you to is often the status quo — a spreadsheet, doing nothing, or an internal hack.

## One profile per competitor

Name files by company (e.g. `acme.md`). Capture:

```markdown
# {{Competitor}}
URL: {{link}} · Category: {{incumbent / startup / DIY / status-quo}} · Funding: {{$}}

## What they do & for whom
## Pricing (with source + date)
## Strengths / Weaknesses (from the customer's POV)
## What customers say (G2/Capterra/Reddit quotes + ratings)
## Gaps we can exploit
```

## The matrix

Maintain `matrix.md` with two views:

1. **Feature/capability matrix** — competitors × the capabilities the persona cares about.
2. **Positioning map (2×2)** — axes = the persona's top two purchase priorities; plot every
   competitor and the status quo, and show where you win.

See the `competitive-analysis` skill for the full method, and the `competitor-analyst`
agent to research a competitor set automatically.
