# Interviews — Primary Market Research

This is the most valuable folder in the venture. Disciplined Entrepreneurship is built on
direct, inquiry-based conversations with potential customers — not surveys, not your own
assumptions. **Get out of the building.**

## Rules (the short version)

- **Inquiry, not advocacy.** You are there to learn, not to sell or validate your ego.
  If you catch yourself pitching, stop and ask a question instead.
- **Ask about their life and past behaviour, not your idea.** "Walk me through the last
  time you faced X" beats "Would you use a product that does Y?" (people lie to be nice).
- **Don't ask hypotheticals or fish for compliments.** Anchor on specifics: what they did,
  when, how much it cost them, what they tried, what they paid.
- **Talk less, listen more.** Aim for the customer talking ~80% of the time.
- **No leading questions.** Avoid "Don't you hate it when…". Ask open, neutral questions.

See the `market-research` skill for the full method, question bank, and synthesis process.

## One note per interview

Name files `YYYY-MM-DD-firstname-role.md`. Capture at minimum:

```markdown
# Interview — {{Name}}, {{Role}} at {{Company}}
Date: {{YYYY-MM-DD}} · Segment: {{segment}} · Persona fit: {{yes/partial/no}}

## Context
Who they are, their situation, why they fit the target.

## What I heard (verbatim where possible)
- Pain: …
- Current workaround / what they use today: …
- What it costs them (time / money / risk): …
- Triggers & decision process: who else is involved, budget, timing.
- Surprises / things that contradicted my assumptions: …

## Quotes
> "…"

## Implications
Which steps/assumptions this confirms, challenges, or changes.
```

After ~10–15 conversations in a segment, synthesize patterns into
`01-who-is-your-customer.md` (end-user profile, persona) and `assumptions.md`.
