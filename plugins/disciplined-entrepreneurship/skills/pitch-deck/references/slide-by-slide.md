# Pitch Deck — Slide by Slide (with the DE source map)

The canonical investor sequence, synthesizing Sequoia, Y Combinator, Guy Kawasaki, and DocSend
evidence. Each slide names what it must contain and which Disciplined Entrepreneurship output feeds it.

| # | Slide | Must contain | Fed by DE step(s) |
|---|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Company purpose** | The company in one declarative sentence — instantly legible | Beachhead (2) + Core (10), distilled |
| 2 | **Problem** | The customer's pain, how it's solved today, why that's inadequate | 1–3, 6 (segmentation, persona, use case) |
| 3 | **Solution** | Your value proposition — unique, compelling, durable | 7–8 (product spec, quantified value) |
| 4 | **Why now** | The recent shift that makes this possible *now* | Market trends |
| 5 | **Market size** | **Bottoms-up** beachhead TAM (+ follow-on); SAM/SOM | 4 (beachhead TAM), 14 (follow-on) |
| 6 | **Product** | How it works — screens/demo/use cases; one idea, minimal text | 6–7; MVBP (22) |
| 7 | **Business model** | How you make money: pricing, revenue streams, unit economics | 15–17, 19 |
| 8 | **Traction** | Evidence: revenue, growth, usage, retention, pipeline, LOIs | 23 ("dogs eat dog food"), 9 (commitments) |
| 9 | **Competition** | Direct + indirect on a 2×2/matrix + your durable edge; never "none" | 11 (competitive position) |
| 10 | **Go-to-market** | Sales/marketing motion + acquisition channels | 12–13, 18 (DMU, process, sales) |
| 11 | **Team** | Founders + key hires and *why you specifically* win | — (external to the 24 steps) |
| 12 | **Financials** | 3–5 yr projection + assumptions; credible bottoms-up | 4, 17, 19 rolled into a model |
| 13 | **The ask / use of funds** | Amount, milestones it buys, runway (Sequoia adds a 5-yr vision) | 24 (product plan) milestones |

## Templates to know

- **Guy Kawasaki 10/20/30** — exactly 10 slides, 20 minutes, no font under 30pt. *"A normal human
  being cannot comprehend more than ten concepts in a meeting."*
- **Sequoia** — ~10 slides (purpose, problem, solution, why-now, market, product, business model,
  team, financials, vision); detail goes to an appendix.
- **Y Combinator** — 10–12 slides; three principles: **legibility, simplicity, obviousness** (large
  bold high-contrast type, one idea per slide, key text at the top, "get it" in seconds).

## DocSend evidence (what investors actually do)

- **Time on deck:** ~**3 min 44 sec** on a first view (cold reads shorter, warm longer); ~**20 sec per
  slide**.
- **Per-slide attention (most → least):** **Team → Financials → Traction →** Problem → Solution →
  Business model → Market → Competition → Product. The **team slide is the most-viewed** (the
  fundamental bet); **financials** is second-most scrutinized — neither can be weak.
- **Reading order matters:** founders who open with the first sections in the recommended order
  (Company Purpose → Problem → Solution → Why-now/Market) raise more often. Attention is front-loaded.
- **Length:** successful seed decks average ~**19 pages**, ~50 words/slide (appendix folds in detail).
- **Raising a seed (benchmark):** ~**58 investors contacted, ~40 meetings, ~12.5 weeks to close**.
  Cold decks → meeting at ~**3–5%**; warm intros at ~**40–50%**. The deck must stand alone *and* you
  should pursue warm intros.

## Fatal mistakes (re-check before sending)

1. No clear ask (amount / milestones / runway).
2. Vague or top-down-only TAM ("1% of a $50B market") with no bottoms-up build.
3. Text-heavy / illegible (breaks 30pt; multiple ideas per slide).
4. No traction or evidence.
5. Weak team slide — fatal, it's the most-viewed.
6. "We have no competitors."
7. Hockey-stick financials with no stated assumptions (second-most scrutinized).
8. Burying the lede — wasting front-loaded attention.
9. Too many slides; no "why now"; unclear business model / missing unit economics.

## Turning DE outputs into slides

The quantified DE work *is* the credible core of the deck: Step 4/14 TAM → the market slide;
Step 8 → the value on the solution slide; Step 11 → the competition 2×2; Steps 15–17,19 → the
business-model and financials slides; Step 23 → the traction slide. If those steps aren't done, the
deck will have holes investors probe first — finish the validation, then assemble.
