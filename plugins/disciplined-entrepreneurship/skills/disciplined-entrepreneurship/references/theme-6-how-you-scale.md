# Theme 6 — How Do You Scale Your Business? (Step 24)

The MVBP works and the dogs are eating the dog food. Now convert validated learning into a
prioritized plan to build out the beachhead product and sequence expansion.

---

## Step 24 — Develop a Product Plan

**Question:** Given what you've validated, what is the prioritized roadmap to complete the beachhead
product and sequence entry into follow-on markets? This converts the MVBP and its feedback into a
**living, multi-market plan** — the bridge from validated startup to scaling company.

**Tool — a product plan / roadmap (a living document) with two layers:**
- **Beachhead build-out** — enhance the MVBP with feature priorities drawn from real Step 23 feedback;
  maintain quality; fix discovered issues; reach a full product the beachhead **loves**.
- **Follow-on strategy** — sequence entry into the Step 14 follow-on markets, ideally by running a
  **mini-version of the 24 Steps** for each new market/persona. **Stagger** expansions so you keep
  serving beachhead customers excellently while funding new growth (the bowling pins fall in order).

**How to do it:** prioritize features by customer value and strategic fit → plot a timeline tied to
the follow-on sequence → keep the beachhead the priority until it is genuinely won → revisit the plan
as the market and learning evolve.

**Common mistakes:** abandoning the beachhead too early to chase expansion; treating the plan as fixed
rather than iterative; expanding before the core product is loved; skipping the mini-24-Steps
discipline for each new market.

## From strategy to execution

The 24 Steps produce a **validated strategy**. Execution — building, marketing, selling, financing,
hiring — is the domain of the companion book, *Disciplined Entrepreneurship: Startup Tactics* (Paul
Cheek & Bill Aulet, 2024): **15 tactics in 4 stages** (Foundations → Market Testing → Product
Development → Resource Acquisition). Its mantra — **"test before you build"** — extends the same
discipline: stand up landing pages, customer lists, and scrappy prototypes to keep generating
evidence before committing engineering. When the founder is ready to raise, route to the `pitch-deck`
skill, which maps these validated outputs onto investor slides.
