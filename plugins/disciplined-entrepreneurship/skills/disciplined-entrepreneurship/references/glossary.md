# DE Glossary

Quick definitions of the framework's load-bearing vocabulary.

- **Beachhead market** — the single, narrow, homogeneous market you attack and dominate first before
  expanding (the D-Day metaphor). Typically a ~$20–100M/year TAM.
- **Bowling-pin strategy** — winning the beachhead (head pin) topples adjacent follow-on markets via
  reusable product, references, and word of mouth.
- **COCA (Cost of Customer Acquisition)** — total sales & marketing spend ÷ new customers acquired,
  over a period. Falls over time. Must satisfy LTV ≥ 3×COCA. See `formulas.md`.
- **Core** — the one thing you do better than anyone, hard to replicate, that grows stronger over
  time. Internal and durable. Different from a moat and from your product. (Step 10)
- **Competitive Position** — how the customer ranks you vs. competitors and "do nothing" on their top
  two priorities. External, customer-facing. (Step 11)
- **DMU (Decision-Making Unit)** — everyone involved in the purchase: champion, end user, primary
  economic buyer, influencers, veto power, purchasing. (Step 12)
- **End User** — the person who actually *uses* the product (not necessarily the buyer). (Step 3)
- **End User Profile** — the typical/aggregate description of the end user. (Step 3)
- **Full Life Cycle Use Case** — the 10-stage map of how the persona discovers → evaluates → acquires
  → installs → uses → values → pays → supports → expands/advocates. (Step 6)
- **Gross margin** — (price − cost of goods) ÷ price. LTV is built on gross *profit*, not revenue.
- **Inquiry, not advocacy** — the PMR stance: learn, never pitch.
- **Lighthouse customer** — an early marquee reference customer given flexible early terms in exchange
  for validation, testimonials, and word of mouth.
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)** — the net present value of profits from one customer over years 0–5.
  Excludes COCA. See `formulas.md`. (Step 17)
- **Moat** — a barrier that slows competitors but can be overcome; buys time to strengthen the Core.
  Not the same as a Core.
- **MVBP (Minimum Viable Business Product)** — the smallest product that **delivers value, captures
  value (the customer pays), and starts a feedback loop**. Stricter than a generic MVP. (Step 22)
- **Persona** — one specific, named, real person who best exemplifies the End User Profile; carries the
  **ranked purchasing criteria** that drive every later step. (Step 5)
- **PMR (Primary Market Research)** — direct first-hand research with potential customers (interviews,
  observation, immersion). See `primary-market-research.md`.
- **Purchasing criteria** — the persona's prioritized list of what matters in a buying decision
  (#1, #2, #3 …). The #1 drives the value prop and brochure; the top two become the Step 11 axes.
- **Quantified Value Proposition** — the as-is vs. possible-state gap, in the persona's #1-priority
  units. (Step 8)
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)** — annual revenue at 100% market share = (# end users) × (annual
  revenue per end user). DE uses a **bottoms-up** estimate for a deliberately **focused** beachhead.
  (Steps 4, 14)
- **"The dogs will eat the dog food"** — real, behavioral, paid evidence of product-market fit (usage,
  retention, referrals), not survey intent. (Step 23)
- **Whole product** — the complete solution (including complements/partners) a customer needs to get
  the promised benefit, not just your piece of it.
- **Window of opportunity / trigger** — the timing and the catalyst that make a customer act now.
